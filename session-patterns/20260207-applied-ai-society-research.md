```suggestion:global
## Competitive Research Analysis

When analyzing competitors, market comparables, or positioning examples for a project:

**Ask first:** What about their specific business model makes this work for them? Does our model share that characteristic?

**Extract principles, not tactics:**
- Good: "They position as 'no hype' which works because the market is saturated with AI hype. We should own similar anti-hype positioning."
- Bad: "They have three personas (engineers, business owners, tool builders). We should segment our messaging the same way."

**Business model differences matter:**
- Community-based vs B2B service
- Open vs closed/paid
- Individual practitioners vs enterprise teams
- Self-serve vs done-for-you

Don't copy positioning that works *because* of a different business model. Extract the underlying principle and apply it to the actual model.

**When the user corrects your analysis:** That's a signal you over-indexed on surface tactics instead of extractable principles. Simplify to just what's genuinely transferable.
```
