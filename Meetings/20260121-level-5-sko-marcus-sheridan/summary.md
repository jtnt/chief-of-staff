### Foundational Mindset: Student to Mastery
The learning progression for any new sales technique:
1. **Be curious** - Open to learning despite experience
2. **Understand concepts** - Clear structure presented
3. **Copy exactly** - Yes, it feels scripted at first
4. **Comprehension** - Understand the principle
5. **Customize** - Make it your own while keeping core principles
6. **Automate** - Practice until it becomes natural as breathing
7. **Teach** - Mastery achieved when you can train others

**Key principle:** Don't skip the "scripted" phase. Crawl → Walk → Run.

### Real Role Plays (Not Fake Role Plays)
Rules for effective role-play training:
- Don't wait to be picked - volunteer
- Treat it as real (high stakes, important client)
- Get into character immediately, no fluff
- Stay in character even when uncomfortable
- Show, don't just tell - maximize reps, minimize discussion
- Respect the room - add energy, help each other win
- Attitude toward role plays = quality of current/previous sales leadership

### Core Technique #1: The Honest Agreement

**Definition:** A mutual commitment about how a conversation will be handled so both parties can be honest, direct, and challenge each other while making clear decisions without defensiveness or hidden resistance.

**The Problem It Solves:** Ghosting
- Buyers like you but can't be honest with you
- They don't want to "tell you your baby's ugly"
- Results in: slow responses, canceled meetings, dead silence
- Root cause: Lack of clarity on priority, decision process, fit

**How to Set an Honest Agreement:**
Framework for opening sales conversations to create psychological safety for directness.

### Core Technique #2: The Pushback Pivot

**Definition:** A technique for handling objections, concerns, or negative statements without getting defensive.

**The Formula:**
1. **Match energy** to the moment
2. **Repeat the exact negative words/friction point** they used
3. **Ask them to explain more** ("Tell me more," "Please explain that," "Help me understand")

**Examples practiced:**
- "The service tech was rude" → "Rude. Tell me more."
- "This is expensive" → "Expensive. Tell me more."
- "Delivery schedule doesn't align" → "Doesn't align. Please explain that."
- "Competitor had bigger model at cheaper price" → "Bigger model at cheaper price. Tell me more about that."

**Why It Works:**
- Prevents defensive reactions
- Shows sincere curiosity about their meaning
- Gives buyer space to fully explain concerns
- Keeps you leading the conversation
- Works in sales, leadership 1:1s, and personal conversations

**Critical Success Factor:** Must have sincere desire to understand their intent. Without sincerity, it fails.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Labeling techniques as "unnatural" or "not me" (progress killer)
- Trying to skip the scripted learning phase
- Getting defensive when buyers express concerns
- Not knowing your team's individual communication challenges
- Doing sales meetings instead of sales training (training = practice + coaching)
- Cognitive overload (trying to teach too much at once)
