### 0.3 The Thesis

**"AI Crossed 50% Win Rate Against Industry Experts. The Constraint Shifted From Expertise to Context."**

#### The GDPval Study (September 2025)

The first rigorous measurement of AI versus industry experts across 57 professional domains. Methodology: blind evaluation by specialist panels. The metric is "win rate" — the percentage of time evaluators preferred AI output without knowing its origin.

#### Win Rate Progression

| Period | Model | Win Rate |
|--------|-------|----------|
| Early 2024 | GPT-4o | 12.4% |
| Mid 2024 | — | 24.3% |
| Mid 2024 | — | 25.5% |
| Mid 2024 | — | 27.9% |
| Late 2024 | — | 34.1% |
| Late 2024 | — | 38.8% |
| Late 2024 | — | 47.6% |
| December 2025 | GPT-5.2 | 70.9% |

#### The 50% Threshold — A Phase Change

**Below 50% (AI as accelerant):** AI functions as a draft accelerator. Outputs require expert review and correction. Experts remain essential for finishing and quality assurance. AI speeds up the process but doesn't replace judgment.

**Above 50% (AI as expert-level producer):** AI produces deliverable-quality work directly. "The finishing problem disappears. The baseline quality is high enough that work can ship without domain-expert intervention." GPT-5.2 operates 20 points beyond parity.

**The critical shift:** "The constraint is no longer expertise. It is context."

### 0.7 The Path

**"The Skills, The Stages, The Progression."**

Becoming a Full-Stack Consultant is "not a weekend certification. It is a discipline."

#### Foundation: The AI Improvement Acceleration Cycle

Lean principles applied to AI workflow development, operating at the process level:

1. Identify a process
2. Document it
3. Augment with AI
4. Secure against edge cases
5. Execute at scale

"The skill transfers across every process, systematically expanding your capability one workflow at a time."

#### Three Core Skills

**Creative Thinking:** Generates possibilities — "the ability to see where AI might apply and imagine workflows that don't yet exist."

**Critical Thinking:** Evaluates output — "the ability to assess whether AI-generated work meets the required standard."

**Systems Thinking:** Connects components — "the ability to see how individual workflows combine into delivery systems that scale."

#### Five Maturity Stages — UCASE Framework

**1. Understanding**
Examine the process as it currently exists. Learn AI capabilities in that domain. Develop intuition for where AI performs well versus where it struggles.

**2. Codification**
Document the process as a Standard Operating Procedure with explicit instructions for execution. "SOPs are the raw material for everything that follows. Without clear documentation, AI has nothing to augment."

**3. Augmentation**
Bring AI into the process by rebuilding the SOP with context engineering principles. The rebuilt workflow "forces the user to generate proper context as they execute. It asks the right questions, compiles the right inputs, and structures the information so AI can perform. This is where the leverage begins."

**4. Securing**
Embed edge cases, exceptions, and guardrails to make processes reliable enough for non-experts. A secure workflow "handles the variations that would otherwise require specialist judgment."

**5. Execution**
Transform the secure workflow into a full agent — "an AI system that executes autonomously within defined parameters, escalating only when it encounters situations outside its boundaries."

#### Compounding Effect

A consultant with a dozen processes at Augmentation operates at multiples of previous capacity. A consultant with processes at Execution operates at multiples again. The progression compounds.

> **Note:** In the Training Stack visualization, the stages are presented as SCOPE: Study, Codify, Operationalize, Protect, Execute — a slightly different acronym for the same five-stage concept.
